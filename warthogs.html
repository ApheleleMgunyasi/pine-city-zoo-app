Scientific Classification

  • Species: P. africanus (Common Warthog), P. aethiopicus (Desert Warthog)
  • Family: Suidae (pig family)
  • Order: Artiodactyla

Habitat & Range

Warthogs are native to sub-Saharan Africa and thrive in savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands. They often use abandoned burrows (like those of aardvarks) for shelter and protection.

Diet

Warthogs are omnivores. They graze on grasses during the wet season and dig for roots, bulbs, and tubers in the dry season. They may also eat insects, eggs, and carrion.

Behavior

  • Active during cooler parts of the day (diurnal)
  • Use burrows for sleeping and raising young
  • Often kneel on front legs while feeding
  • Roll in mud to cool off and protect skin from insects

Conservation Status

Warthogs are listed as Least Concern by the IUCN. Their adaptability and wide range help maintain stable populations, though habitat loss and hunting can pose local threats.
