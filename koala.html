KOALA

Koalas are arboreal marsupials native to Australia, often mistakenly called "koala bears." They are not bears, but are closely related to wombats.

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: 60–85 cm (24–33 inches) long.
  • Weight: 4–15 kg (8.8–33.1 lbs).
  • Fur: Thick, wool-like fur ranging from silver-grey to chocolate brown.
  • Large, fluffy ears and a broad, leathery nose.
  • Strong claws for gripping tree branches.

Habitat & Behavior

  • Found in eucalyptus forests across Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, and South Australia.
  • Sleep up to 20 hours a day due to their low-energy diet.
  • Koalas are solitary and mainly bond with their offspring.
  • Communicate using deep bellows and scent markings.

Diet

  • Exclusively eat eucalyptus leaves, consuming up to 1.3 kg (3 lbs) daily.
  • Their digestive system neutralizes toxins found in eucalyptus.
  • Rarely drink water, getting most hydration from leaves.

Reproduction

  • Koalas give birth to tiny, underdeveloped joeys that crawl into the mother's pouch.
  • Joeys stay in the pouch for 6–7 months before emerging.
  • They are fully weaned around one year old.

Conservation Status

  • Koalas are classified as Vulnerable due to habitat destruction, disease, and climate change.
  • Threats include bushfires, deforestation, and urban expansion.
  • Chlamydia infections are widespread in some populations, causing infertility.

WHERE WILL I FIND THEM?

Where to Find Koalas at Pine City Zoo?

At Pine City Zoo, koalas are located in the Australian Wildlife Exhibit, near the Eucalyptus Grove.

  • Koalas can be found in **Habitat Zone K12**, designed to mimic their natural environment.
  • Visitors can see them **resting on eucalyptus trees** or **interacting with caretakers** during feeding times.
  • Educational sessions about koalas are held at the **Wildlife Learning Center**.
