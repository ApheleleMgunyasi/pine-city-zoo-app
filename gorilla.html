GORILLA

Gorillas are closely related to humans, sharing 96–99% of our DNA. They are herbivorous and inhabit the tropical forests of equatorial Africa.

Gorilla Species

  • Western Gorilla (Gorilla gorilla) – Includes the Western Lowland Gorilla and Cross River Gorilla.
  • Eastern Gorilla (Gorilla beringei) – Includes the Eastern Lowland Gorilla and Mountain Gorilla.

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: Males can reach 1.8 meters (5 ft 11 in) in height and weigh up to 270 kg (600 lbs).
  • Arm Span: Up to 2.6 meters (8 ft 6 in).
  • Silverbacks: Adult males develop silver-grey hair on their backs.
  • Fingerprints: Like humans, gorillas have unique fingerprints.

Habitat & Behavior

  • Live in troops led by a dominant silverback.
  • Knuckle-walk using their fists for support.
  • Build nests for sleeping, usually on the ground.
  • Lifespan ranges from 35–70 years in the wild.

Conservation Status

  • Gorillas face threats like poaching, habitat destruction, and disease.
  • The Mountain Gorilla is classified as Endangered.
  • Other subspecies are Critically Endangered.
