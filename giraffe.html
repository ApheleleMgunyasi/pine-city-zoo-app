GIRAFFE

Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: Males can reach 5.5 meters (18 feet), females up to 4.5 meters (15 feet).
  • Weight: Males: 1,930 kg (4,250 lbs), Females: 1,180 kg (2,600 lbs).
  • Neck: Despite its long neck, the giraffe has only seven vertebrae, like most mammals.
  • Horns (Ossicones): Both sexes have horn-like structures covered in skin.

Habitat & Zoo Location

Giraffes are found across Pine city zoo and grasslands. At Pine City Zoo, they are housed in the Savanna Heights Exhibit, which includes elevated platforms for visitors to see them up close.

Diet & Adaptations

  • Giraffes primarily eat leaves, fruits, and flowers from tall trees.
  • They use their 45 cm (18-inch) long prehensile tongue to grasp leaves.
  • They can go weeks without drinking water, getting moisture from food.

Behavior & Conservation

  • Giraffes live in loose social groups, often led by a dominant male.
  • They communicate using low-frequency sounds and body language.
  • Listed as Vulnerable by the IUCN, with conservation efforts focusing on protected reserves.
