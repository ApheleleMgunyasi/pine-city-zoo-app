Classification & Types

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Class: Mammalia
  • Order: Primates
  • Infraorder: Simiiformes

Monkeys are split into two main groups:

  • Old World Monkeys: Africa & Asia (e.g., baboons, macaques)
  • New World Monkeys: Central & South America (e.g., capuchins, howler monkeys)

Habitat

Monkeys inhabit tropical rainforests, savannas, mountains, and even urban areas. Most are tree-dwelling (arboreal), but some like baboons are ground dwellers (terrestrial).

Intelligence & Behavior

  • Use of tools for problem-solving
  • Social learning and communication (vocalizations, gestures)
  • Strong bonds within groups or troops

Diet

Monkeys are omnivores. Their diet includes:

  • Fruits, leaves, and seeds
  • Insects and eggs
  • Occasionally small animals

New World monkeys often have prehensile tails for grabbing and climbing.

Mothers care for young, teaching them through observation and imitation.

Physical Traits

Size varies from 15 cm (pygmy marmosets) to over 1 meter (mandrills). Most have tails and five-fingered hands and feet for climbing and manipulation.

Conservation

Many monkey species face threats such as:

  • Deforestation
  • Illegal pet trade
  • Climate change and hunting

Conservation efforts focus on habitat protection and species recovery.
