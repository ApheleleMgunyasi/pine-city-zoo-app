ELEPHANTS

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, known for their **intelligence, strong social bonds, and incredible memory**. They have thick, gray skin, large ears that help regulate body temperature, and a long, flexible trunk used for drinking, grabbing objects, and communicating. There are two main species: **African elephants** (larger with bigger ears) and **Asian elephants** (smaller with rounded ears). Elephants are herbivores, feeding on grass, leaves, bark, and roots. They play a crucial role in their ecosystems by shaping landscapes and creating water sources for other animals.

Size:Elephants are massive creatures! An adult **African elephant** can weigh between **4,500 to 6,800 kg (10,000 to 15,000 lbs)** and stand **up to 4 meters (13 feet) tall**. **Asian elephants** are slightly smaller, weighing **2,700 to 5,400 kg (6,000 to 12,000 lbs)** and reaching **3.5 meters (11.5 feet) in height**.
