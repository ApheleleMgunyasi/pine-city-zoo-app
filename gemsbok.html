GEMSBOK

Scientific Name: Oryx gazella

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: 1.2 meters (4 feet) at the shoulder, body length of 190-240 cm (75-94 inches).
  • Weight: Males: 180-240 kg (400-530 lbs), Females: 100-210 kg (220-460 lbs).
  • Horns: Both sexes have long, straight horns, averaging 85 cm (33 inches).
  • Coloration: Light tan fur with black-and-white facial markings.

Habitat & Zoo Location

Gemsbok are native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, including deserts and savannas. At Pine City Zoo, they are housed in the Desert Giants Exhibit, an enclosure designed to replicate their natural environment with rocky terrain and sparse vegetation.

Diet & Adaptations

  • Gemsbok primarily graze on dry grasses but will consume roots and tubers when necessary.
  • They can survive long periods without drinking water by extracting moisture from food.

Behavior & Conservation

  • Gemsbok live in herds of 10-40 individuals, led by a dominant male.
  • They are territorial and defend their space using sharp horns.
  • Listed as Least Concern by the IUCN, meaning they are not currently at risk of extinction.
