Social Structure

Lions live in groups called prides made up of related females, their cubs, and one or more dominant males.

Diet & Hunting

Lions are apex predators that hunt wildebeest, zebras, antelope, and more. Females usually lead cooperative hunting efforts.

Behavior

They are mostly active at night or during twilight. Lions rest for up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy.

Conservation Status

Lions are listed as Vulnerable due to habitat loss, human conflict, and poaching. Their population has dropped by 40% since the 1990s.

Cultural Significance

Symbolizing strength and courage, lions appear in myths, emblems, and stories throughout history.
